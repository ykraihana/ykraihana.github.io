The Discovery of DNA: Stop-Motion Animation

This project is a stop-motion animation tracking the history and discovery of DNA. The animation focuses on the critical scientific contributions of Rosalind Franklin, Francis Crick, and James Watson in unlocking the structure of the double helix.

Project Overview & Production

To create this animation, the process involved writing a detailed script, building physical models to represent the molecular structures, and capturing individual frames step-by-step. The final images were compiled and edited together using Adobe Premiere to create a smooth, educational video timeline.

Key Topics Covered

  • The structure of the DNA double helix
  • Rosalind Franklin's Photo 51 and her work in X-ray diffraction
  • Mendelian genetics, hereditary traits, and molecular biology history
