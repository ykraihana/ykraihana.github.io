My Screenprinting Project

For this manufacturing and design project, I went through the entire hands-on process of building a custom screenprinting frame from scratch and preparing a digital design to be printed.

Project Details & Process

The project began in the woodshop, where I measured, marked, and cut wood down to size using the miter saw. After cutting the pieces, I glued and stapled the wood corners together to form a solid frame. Next, I prepped the screen by cutting, stretching, stapling, hammering, and taping the fine mesh tightly across the frame.

For the graphic, I used a vinyl cutting machine to cut and weed a stencil of my digital design. Finally, I used a heat transfer press to iron the vinyl stencil directly onto the mesh, and used tape around the edges to block any unwanted ink leaks during the printing process.

Key Materials & Steps

  • Measuring, marking, and cutting wood with a miter saw
  • Gluing and stapling wood corners to build the frame
  • Cutting, stretching, stapling, hammering, and taping mesh to the frame
  • Cutting and weeding a digital design stencil with a vinyl cutter
  • Using a heat transfer press to iron the stencil to the mesh and taping edges
