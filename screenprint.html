My Screenprinting Project

For this manufacturing and design project, I went through the entire hands-on process of building a custom screenprinting frame from scratch and preparing a digital design to be printed. It was definitely a lot more work than just regular printing, but it gave me a chance to learn how the whole process actually works. Doing everything myself made the final product feel way more rewarding. I'm really glad I got to try out all the different tools to make it happen.

Project Details & Process

The project began in the woodshop, where I measured, marked, and cut wood down to size using the miter saw. After cutting the pieces, I glued and stapled the wood corners together to form a solid frame. Next, I prepped the screen by cutting, stretching, stapling, hammering, and taping the fine mesh tightly across the frame. Making sure the screen was tight enough was probably one of the hardest parts of the whole thing.

For the graphic, I used a vinyl cutting machine to cut and weed a stencil of my digital design. Weeding the vinyl took a lot of focus because you don't want to mess up the clean lines of the image. Finally, I used a heat transfer press to iron the vinyl stencil directly onto the mesh, and used tape around the edges to block any unwanted ink leaks during the printing process. It was awesome to see the final design turn out so clean after all that work.

Key Materials & Steps

  • Measuring, marking, and cutting wood with a miter saw
  • Gluing and stapling wood corners to build the frame
  • Cutting, stretching, stapling, hammering, and taping mesh to the frame
  • Cutting and weeding a digital design stencil with a vinyl cutter
  • Using a heat transfer press to iron the stencil to the mesh and taping edges
